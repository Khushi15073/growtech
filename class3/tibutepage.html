Tribute to Father Of Nation

MAHATMA GANDHI

02 October 1869- 30 January 1948

Mahatma Gandhi (Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi) was a visionary leader, freedom fighter, and social reformer born on October 2, 1869,in Porbandar, India. He is best known for leading India’s struggle for independence from British rule using the powerful principles of non-violence (ahimsa) and truth (satya). Gandhi's method of peaceful protest, called Satyagraha, became a global symbol of resistance against oppression. His movements such as the Salt March (1930)and Quit India Movement (1942) mobilized millions and proved that change could be achieved without violence. He believed in simple living, self-reliance, and spiritual discipline, often wearing hand-spun khadi clothes and promoting the idea of Swadeshi (use of local goods). Gandhiji was tragically assassinated on January 30, 1948, but his legacy lives on. He remains a global icon for peace, human rights, and dignity.

"The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others." – Mahatma Gandhi

In the First World War which took place between 1914 - 1919, Gandhiji gave full support to the British government on the condition that after this they would liberate India. But when the British did not do so, then Gandhiji started a number of movements to liberate the country. Some of these movements are as follows:

  1. In 1920: Non-cooperation movement
  2. In 1930: Disobedience movement
  3. In 1942: Quit India Movement
  4. In1918 : Champaran and Kheda Satyagraha
  5. In1919: Khilafat Movement
